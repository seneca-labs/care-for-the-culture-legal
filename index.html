01

overview

OVERVIEW

the problem

The ask

Get HIV prevention data into Freddie parties without it feeling like a health booth. It has to belong in the room.

The behavior

Phones run the room. In queer nightlife, the apps people use are proximity-based. Faceless profiles. Who's near you, not who lives across town.

The opportunity

Replace that dynamic. The kiosk becomes the entry point. The data becomes the social layer.

STRATEGY

our approach

THE COMMON NARRATIVE

Show people how many HIV cases are near them, and hope that fear motivates action.

OUR NARRATIVE

Show people where their community stands, where the gap is, and how they fit into the picture.

The data is not the destination, but the context we use to make the experience meaningful.

THE DATA

what we can work with

The data comes from AIDSVu.org, broken down by county. For each area we have:

Prevalence

How many people are living with HIV. The full weight of the epidemic in that place.

New diagnoses

What happened this year. What's active right now.

PrEP coverage

Who's on PrEP. How many people in that area are using it.

The gap

The distance between who's getting diagnosed and who's on PrEP. Where the system loses people.

Racial breakdown

Splits by race, sex, age, and transmission category. The disparities are stark.

Personal answers

Each participant's own prevention status. Collected privately, used for follow-up.

The data is not the destination, but the context we use to make the experience meaningful.

OVERVIEW

the solution

Not a kiosk. Not an app. A system.

ENTRY POINT

The kiosk

Physical station at the party. Photos happen here, data gets collected, cards get created. Multiple kiosks across the venue. The ritual.

EXTENSION

The phone

A QR code at the kiosk opens the same feed in your mobile browser. No app download. Browse from the dance floor, the afterparty, or the next morning. The utility.

Both surfaces read from the same backend. A photo taken at the kiosk appears on every phone feed within seconds.

Entry

Take a photo at the kiosk. Only required step.

Layer

Your photo joins the feed. Proximity without the apps.

Takeaway

A tinted photo card, a morning text, social connection, and your local data.

SCALE

tracking roi

Four metrics per event. Each one feeds back into product and strategy.

MetricImpact
Awareness lift % of kiosk users who moved from "what's PrEP" to "heard of it" or higher. Proof the experience itself moves the needle.
Morning text click-through % of opted-in users who tap through to resources the next day, by PrEP answer. Tells us which audiences respond to which messages.
Completion rate % of users who finish the full flow, by PrEP answer. Shows where the product is and isn't working for each audience.
Resource follow-through % of click-throughs that lead to a real action: a Freddie visit, a provider search, a PrEP resource. The hardest and most defensible number.

The audiences who knew the least are the ones we're moving the most. That's the product working, and the data telling us where to push next.

METHODOLOGY

how we calculated everything

Every number traces to the AIDSVu spreadsheet.

The Slider

AIDSVu national PrEP totals, real headcounts from 2012 to 2023. Dot density scales to the count.

The Community Map + Data Card

Prevalence, PrEP, and new diagnoses on one map. Orange dots for HIV, green for PrEP. The data card shows all three with the gap ratio.

The Impact Screen

Three visualization options, each computed differently. The awareness ripple is metaphorical. The county gap maps real AIDSVu prevalence vs PrEP. The identity gap breaks coverage by race, age, and gender.

The Gap Ratio

PrEP rate per 100K divided by new diagnosis rate per 100K. Direct division of two published AIDSVu numbers. Shown inside the data card for each borough.

LEGAL

the data

RiskHow we address it
Rates misread as headcounts "Per 100K" is shown next to every rate.
Dots read as geographic "Proportional, not geographic" appears on every map.
Derived calculations challenged It's a simple division of published AIDSVu data, no AI.

LEGAL

privacy

RiskHow we address it
PrEP status linked to photos The answer is never shown as text, only a color tint.
Photos screenshotted from feed There is an explicit opt-in that says "your card will be visible."
Unsolicited texts TCPA language appears before sending, and the morning text has its own opt-in.
Prevention question feels invasive "Prefer not to say" is equal-sized, not a dismissal.
PrEP status exposed via photo The answer is only shown as a color tint, never as text.
User unaware before engaging The onboarding explains everything before any input.
Card tint reveals answer The color is subtle and the key only appears during the data flow, never on the feed or in print.

LEGAL

retention + claims

RiskHow we address it
Data storage All data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
Purging Photos purged the morning after the event. All PII deleted within 24 hours.
Language We say "aware," never "protected." No second-person health claims.
Claims The impact screen is a metaphor, not a medical efficacy statement.

LEGAL

risk mitigation

Every risk maps to a specific safeguard.

ENCRYPTION

At rest: AES-256 on GCP/AWS.

In transit: TLS 1.2+ for all transfers.

24-HOUR PURGE

Zip codes, PrEP answers, and photos deleted within 24 hours. Phone numbers deleted after morning text.

NO PII CO-LOCATION

Name, zip, photo, and PrEP answer are never stored in the same row or joinable table.

CONTRACTUAL PROTECTIONS

Indemnification: Mutual between Seneca/Vega, Majority, and Gilead.

Liability caps: Standard SaaS terms for event platforms.

INSURANCE

Cyber liability, event insurance, E&O policy covering data handling and third-party claims.

BREACH RESPONSE

Detection: Real-time cloud monitoring.

Notification: Gilead within 24h of confirmed breach.

Forensics: Third-party audit within 48h.

LEGAL

terms of service

Modeled on Google's event ToS pattern. Our team built Google's consent framework for activation-based data collection.

STRUCTURE

Lightweight ToS at consent screen

Scrollable, agree-to-continue. Before any data collection.

Aligned with 24-hour purge

ToS states all data deleted within 24 hours. No archives.

Plain language, not legalese

8th-grade reading level. One-sentence summary per clause.

COVERAGE

Collects: zip, PrEP question, photo, optional phone & social handle.

Never collects: name, email, GPS, device ID, health records.

Retention: 24h max. Photos morning-after. Phone after text.

Opt-out: skip any step, reply STOP, no penalty.

Jurisdiction: Governed by event state. US processing only.
