01

overview

OVERVIEW

the problem

The ask

Get HIV prevention data into Freddie parties without it feeling like a health booth. It has to belong in the room.

The behavior

Phones run the room. In queer nightlife, the apps people use are proximity-based. Faceless profiles. Who's near you, not who lives across town.

The opportunity

Replace that dynamic. The kiosk becomes the entry point. The data becomes the social layer.

STRATEGY

our approach

THE COMMON NARRATIVE

Show people how many HIV cases are near them, and hope that fear motivates action.

OUR NARRATIVE

Show people where their community stands, where the gap is, and how they fit into the picture.

The data is not the destination, but the context we use to make the experience meaningful.

THE DATA

what we can work with

The data comes from AIDSVu.org, broken down by county. For each area we have:

Prevalence

How many people are living with HIV. The full weight of the epidemic in that place.

New diagnoses

What happened this year. What's active right now.

PrEP coverage

Who's on PrEP. How many people in that area are using it.

The gap

The distance between who's getting diagnosed and who's on PrEP. Where the system loses people.

Racial breakdown

Splits by race, sex, age, and transmission category. The disparities are stark.

Personal answers

Each participant's own prevention status. Collected privately, used for follow-up.

The data is not the destination, but the context we use to make the experience meaningful.

05

legal

LEGAL

items we've identified

Frameworks we know apply. We need your team's guidance on scope, process, and ownership.

FDA OPDP, branded vs unbranded

OPDP regulates prescription drug promotion. We're designing this as unbranded disease awareness. We need your read on whether any resource link, SMS, or printed card could cross into branded territory, and what fair balance would look like if it does.

MLR Review

Medical, Legal, Regulatory review of every user-facing surface. We need guidance on submission format, which surfaces require review, and turnaround so we can schedule around it.

Anti-Kickback Statute, the Freddie pathway

Freddie is a telehealth PrEP provider that prescribes your team's products. That makes the pathway sensitive under AKS and the PhRMA Code. We'd like your commercial compliance team's read on whether the photo card, party context, or morning-text resources constitute items of value.

Sunshine Act / Open Payments

We need your team's read on whether any element of the activation (Freddie's medical staff involvement, HCPs at conference deployments, the photo card itself) triggers reporting.

State HIV Confidentiality, NY 27-F and peers

NY Public Health Law Article 27-F applies to the NYC pilot. We need a legal read on whether "I'm on PrEP" falls inside its scope, and a state-by-state memo before expansion.

Biometric Privacy, NYC and LA first

NYC biometric identifier law and California's CPRA apply to the pilot cities. Our photo pipeline applies a color LUT only. No face detection, no landmark extraction, no embeddings. We can provide a technical attestation in whatever form your team prefers.

DPIA, Data Protection Impact Assessment

We've mapped the data flow and risks. We need your privacy team's template and sign-off process.

TCPA, consent standard and ledger

Whether the morning text is informational or marketing under TCPA. An informational text ("here's your photo") and a marketing text (linking to PrEP resources) have different consent standards. Also whether to use your existing consent-ledger vendor or stand up our own.

HIPAA Non-Applicability

Our read is that we are not a covered entity or business associate, and the data collected is not PHI. We'll prepare a position paper articulating the analysis, for your counsel's concurrence.

LEGAL

hiv confidentiality

State HIV laws are stricter than general privacy law. We're taking them seriously.

NY PUBLIC HEALTH LAW 27-F

New York's HIV confidentiality statute applies to the NYC pilot. It defines HIV-related information broadly and carries its own penalties. Whether "I'm on PrEP" falls inside its scope needs a formal legal read from NY healthcare counsel before the pilot.

STATE-BY-STATE MEMO

Before any expansion beyond NYC, we will commission a memo covering HIV confidentiality statutes in every pilot state. CA, TX, FL, and IL at minimum.

OUR POSTURE

We treat PrEP status as protected health information for the purposes of this experience, regardless of whether any given statute technically requires it. That's why the card tint is color only, the answer is never shown as text, and the color key lives only inside the data flow.
